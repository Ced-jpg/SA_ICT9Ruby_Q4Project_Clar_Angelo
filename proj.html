Ferdinand Porsche


Ferdinand Porsche (September 3, 1875 - January 30, 1951) was an influential automotive engineer and entrepreneur. He was born in Maffersdorf, Austria-Hungary (now Czech Republic) and passed away in Stuttgart, Germany.

Porsche is best known for creating the Volkswagen Beetle, a groundbreaking compact car that became an automotive icon. He also designed high-performance racing cars and innovative technologies for companies like Mercedes-Benz and NSU.

His son, Ferry Porsche, went on to establish the renowned Porsche automobile company. Ferdinand Porsche's contributions to the automotive industry continue to impact and inspire to this day.
